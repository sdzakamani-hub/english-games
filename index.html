FOR TEACHERS

Built with Pedagogy in Mind

The game scaffolds knowledge progressively across three rooms — from form recognition, to contextualised editing, to conceptual sequencing. Here's how to use it:

  • ①Use as a post-lesson consolidation activity after teaching each tense, or as a complete revision lesson covering all past tenses.
  • ②The attempt counter on the win screen tells you which rooms caused the most difficulty — use this for targeted follow-up.
  • ③Students can work individually or in pairs. Pair work often generates productive grammar discussion ("is it was walking or walked here?").
  • ④The hint boxes contain reference tables students can revisit. Encourage weaker learners to open them freely — there's no penalty.
  • ⑤The reset button lets students replay immediately — excellent for self-directed revision or homework.

🔎 Past Continuous vs Simple Past

Past Continuous (was/were + verb‑ing): an action that was in progress at a moment in the past.

Simple Past (verb + ‑ed / irregular): a completed action.

✅ "While she was walking to school, she found a key."
✅ "When I arrived, Tom was reading a book."

🏛️ Past Perfect — Quick Reference

Use Past Perfect (had + past participle) when one past action happened before another past action.

📅 Event 1 (earlier): She left.    📅 Event 2 (later): I arrived.
✅ "When I arrived, she had already left."

Signal words: already · just · before · after · by the time · never
